Night Stories-New Orleans is an old school RPG based on pen n' paper games. The outcome of the story is affected by the decisions of the player and uses a ten-sided dice system.

RULES

The game begins by creating your character. You have to assign points to attributes and skills. These points will affect your chances of succeeding or failing when the dice are rolled. The numbers 7,8,9 & 10 indicate success. The number 10 re-rolls the specific dice. For a scene to be successful, you may need more than one successful dice, taking into consideration the difficulty level of the scene, which is not visible to you. The number of dice comes from the sum of the specific attributes and skills depeding on the scene

For Example, your character tries to shoot a target. If you have assigned 3 points to physical attribute and you have chosen the pistol skill, you have a total of 4 dice to roll. Assuming you have rolled 2, 8, 5, 10, you have two successes and number 10 is re-rolled, giving you a 9. You have three successes, totally. The difficulty level of the scene is two. Since you have three successes you complete the scene successfully, providing you with the extra info or letting you accomplish a task.
